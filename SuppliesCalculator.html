15-30% of plastic cutlery is wasted due to single-use and disposal (EPA)

According to various studies and estimates, here are some statistics on extra plastic cutlery wasted by guests due to single-use and disposal in parties or events:

  • 20-30% of guests take extra plastic cutlery (forks, knives, spoons) unnecessarily (WRAP)
  • 15-25% of plastic cutlery is wasted due to over-provisioning (EPA)
  • 10-20% of guests use multiple sets of plastic cutlery per event (Ocean Conservancy)
  • 5-15% of plastic cutlery is littered or discarded incorrectly (Keep America Beautiful)

Breakdown of extra plastic cutlery wasted:

Type of Cutlery Extra Waste (%)
Forks 25-40%
Knives 20-35%
Spoons 15-30%
Other (stirrers, straws) 10-25%

Reasons for extra plastic cutlery waste:

  • Over-provisioning by event organizers
  • Guests taking extra cutlery unnecessarily
  • Lack of awareness about plastic waste
  • Inadequate waste management

To reduce extra plastic cutlery waste:

  • Implement reusable or biodegradable cutlery
  • Provide cutlery only upon request
  • Educate guests about plastic waste
  • Monitor and adjust cutlery provision

Sources:

  • WRAP (Waste and Resources Action Programme)
  • EPA (United States Environmental Protection Agency)
  • Ocean Conservancy
  • Keep America Beautiful
